Fundamental 1 – Chapter 1 – Why Requirement Classification Exists

Coaching Record

Conversation 1 – Why do architects classify requirements?

Coach
Why is requirement classification necessary at all?
Don't explain how to classify requirements. Explain the problem that requirement classification solves.
Student
Requirement classification is important to understand the business needs. It helps us understand the scope of work, bottlenecks, limitations and budget allocation based on the effort required.
Diagnosis
The reasoning correctly focused on the business instead of technology. However, the answer described the benefits rather than the fundamental reason requirement classification exists.
Architect Refinement
Requirement classification exists because architects must understand the business problem before proposing technical solutions. Without understanding the problem, they risk solving the wrong problem exceptionally well.
Architecture Principle
Understand the problem before optimizing the solution.

Conversation 2 – Why spend time understanding requirements?

Coach
Why is an architect who spends five minutes understanding requirements better than one who immediately starts drawing the architecture?
Student
After understanding the requirement, we may discover that we already have a suitable solution or that we cannot provide the requested solution. Therefore, it is better to understand the requirement first.
Diagnosis
Good architect thinking. The answer shifted from implementation toward decision making, but it still focused on consequences rather than the root principle.
Architect Refinement
Architects delay design because early clarification reduces uncertainty. The objective is to avoid making assumptions and solving the wrong problem.
Architecture Principle
Reduce uncertainty before making architectural decisions.

Conversation 3 – The core mental model

Coach
Complete the sentence:
Requirement classification exists because without understanding the problem, we risk ________.
Student
Solving the wrong problem.
Diagnosis
This captured the fundamental purpose of requirement classification and became the central mental model for the chapter.
Architect Refinement
Requirement classification ensures that architecture is driven by business understanding rather than assumptions. Good architecture begins with the right problem, not the right technology.
Architecture Principle
Business understanding precedes technical design.

Learning Capture

  • Shifted focus from technology to business understanding.
  • Realized that requirement classification is a decision-making framework rather than a documentation exercise.
  • Recognized that the biggest architectural risk is solving the wrong problem.
  • Began explaining answers through reasoning instead of isolated statements.
