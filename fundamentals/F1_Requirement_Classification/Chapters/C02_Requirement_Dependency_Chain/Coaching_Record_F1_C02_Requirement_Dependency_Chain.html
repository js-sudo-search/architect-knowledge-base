Fundamental 1 – Chapter 2 – Requirement Dependency Chain

Coaching Record

Conversation 1 – What is a dependency?

Coach
When we say Functional Requirements depend on Business Objectives, what does the word dependency actually mean?
Student
A depends on B because A can make decisions based on the information provided by B.
Diagnosis
The reasoning correctly identified information flow, but the definition was incomplete. A dependency is more than information transfer—it reduces uncertainty and constrains downstream decisions.
Architect Refinement
A depends on B because B provides the context, constraints and clarity required for A to make informed decisions. Without B, A relies on assumptions rather than evidence.
Architecture Principle
Dependencies exist to reduce uncertainty before decisions are made.

Conversation 2 – Information vs Constraints

Coach
Does only information flow downward through the dependency chain, or do constraints also flow downward?
Student
Both information and constraints flow downward.
Diagnosis
Correct reasoning. The discussion expanded the meaning of dependency from information sharing to decision boundaries.
Architect Refinement
Every upstream decision establishes the context and constraints for downstream decisions. Architects inherit these constraints rather than redefining them at every stage.
Architecture Principle
Context, information and constraints propagate through the dependency chain.

Conversation 3 – Why does the order matter?

Coach
Why can't we change the order of the framework? Why isn't it just a checklist?
Student
Each stage influences the next one. Downstream decisions rely on upstream information to avoid assumptions.
Diagnosis
Good reasoning. The understanding shifted from memorizing a sequence to recognizing causal relationships.
Architect Refinement
The framework is a dependency graph, not a checklist. Every layer answers a different architectural question: 
Business Objective
        ↓
Functional Requirements
        ↓
Non-Functional Requirements
        ↓
Scale Estimation
        ↓
Architecture
Removing or reordering a layer increases uncertainty and leads to weaker architectural decisions.
Architecture Principle
Architectures are built on dependencies, not checklists.
