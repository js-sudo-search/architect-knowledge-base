Articulation Upgrade – Chapter 6

Problem 1

Why does geographic scope matter?

Original: Global deployment affects server count, availability and redundancy.

Diagnosis: Skipped the causal chain.

Architect Version: Geographic distribution primarily increases network latency. That motivates regional deployments and traffic routing. Multi-region deployments also improve resilience by enabling regional failover.

Problem 2

Why perform scale estimation before architecture?

Diagnosis: Focus drifted toward NFRs instead of evidence gathering.

Architect Version: I estimate workload characteristics such as QPS, storage growth and throughput before proposing an architecture because those estimates determine whether a simple or distributed design is appropriate.

Problem 3

Which metrics would you estimate first?

Diagnosis: Mixed inputs with derived metrics.

Architect Version: I would estimate read/write QPS, storage growth, throughput requirements and peak versus average load. Each directly informs a scaling, storage, networking or capacity-planning decision.
