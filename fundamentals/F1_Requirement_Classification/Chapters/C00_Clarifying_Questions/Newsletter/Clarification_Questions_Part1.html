Fundamental 1

Clarification Questions (CQ)

Architects don't start by designing. They start by removing ambiguity.

What are Clarification Questions?

Clarification Questions are the questions an architect asks before identifying requirements. Their purpose is to understand the problem, eliminate ambiguity, and establish the correct scope before making design decisions.

Architect Insight
The quality of your architecture is bounded by the quality of your questions.

Coach → Student

Coach: Design WhatsApp.
Student: I'll start listing Functional Requirements.
Coach: Are you sure you know what problem you're solving? Consumer or enterprise? Is media sharing required? End-to-end encryption? Scale?

Articulation Upgrade

Before: "We should ask some questions first."

After: "Before identifying functional requirements, an architect removes ambiguity through clarification questions. The answers define the scope, assumptions, and influence every architectural decision that follows."

Common Mistakes

  • Jumping directly to architecture.
  • Treating assumptions as facts.
  • Asking implementation questions instead of business questions.
  • Asking every possible question instead of high-impact ones.

Final Learning

  • CQ is the first step in a real system design interview.
  • Architects remove ambiguity before discussing requirements.
  • Good clarification questions reduce rework and improve design quality.
  • The goal is not to ask many questions, but the right questions.
