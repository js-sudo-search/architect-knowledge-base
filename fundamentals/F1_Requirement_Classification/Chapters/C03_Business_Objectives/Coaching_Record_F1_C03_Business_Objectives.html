Fundamental 1 – Chapter 3 – Business Objectives
Coaching Record

Conversation 1 – Business Objective vs Solution

Coach
Product Manager says: "We need to build a notification service."

Would you accept this as the Business Objective?
Student
The sentence is missing crucial information.
  1. We do not know whether it is an internal request or a new business request.
  2. It does not provide sufficient context or constraints.
Diagnosis
Good observation regarding lack of context. However, the reasoning still accepted "Notification Service" as the starting point. The deeper architect question is whether it is the business objective or already a proposed solution.
Architect Refinement
An architect should identify whether the stakeholder is describing:
  • Business Problem
  • Business Objective
  • Proposed Solution
A notification service is one possible solution.
Architecture Principle
Architects validate the problem before validating the solution.
Newsletter Opportunity
The Product Manager Already Gave You a Solution.

Conversation 2 – Business Objective Discovery

Coach
Users are complaining that they don't know when their order has been shipped.
What is the Business Objective?
Student
The business objective is to give timely shipment updates.
Diagnosis
Good transition away from technology, but still close to the solution. Continue asking "Why?" until reaching the business outcome.
Architect Refinement
Improve customer experience → Reduce uncertainty → Increase trust → Improve retention. Shipment updates are one possible implementation.
Architecture Principle
Business objectives describe outcomes. Solutions implement outcomes.

Conversation 3 – Business Objective vs Metric

Coach
PM A: Increase user engagement.
PM B: Increase session duration by 20%.
Which is the better business objective?
Student
PM B is more measurable. My next question would be: What business value does increasing session duration provide?
Diagnosis
Excellent reasoning. You moved naturally from metric to business value.
Architect Refinement
Business Objective → Success Metric → Target.
Metrics measure objectives; they are not objectives.
Architecture Principle
Architects optimize objectives. Metrics indicate progress.

Conversation 4 – Conflicting Business Objectives

Coach
CEO: Increase customer satisfaction.
CFO: Reduce infrastructure cost.
Product: Launch three premium features.
Student
The objectives have dependencies and conflicts. We should prioritize based on business value, budget, dependencies and conflicts.
Diagnosis
Excellent identification of conflicts. The next level is to optimize multiple objectives before sacrificing one.
Architect Refinement
Architects optimize overall business value rather than individual objectives.
Architecture Principle
Trade-offs should be the last option, not the first.

Learning Capture

  • Shifted from feature thinking to business value thinking.
  • Began asking "What business value?" instead of "What feature?".
  • Distinguished Business Objective, Solution, Metric and Business Value.
  • Reasoned through dependencies, constraints and trade-offs.
