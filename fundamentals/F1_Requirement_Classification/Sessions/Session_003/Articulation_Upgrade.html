Articulation Upgrade – Chapter C05: Non-Functional Requirements

Purpose
Capture how interview answers evolved from technically correct responses into architect-level communication. The focus is not only on correctness, but on how to present reasoning in a structured and professional manner.

Problem 1 – Conflicting Non-Functional Requirements

Interview Problem

A Product Manager asks for:
  • 99.999% availability
  • <100 ms latency
  • Zero data loss
  • Lowest infrastructure cost
How would you respond?

Your Original Answer (Mic)

Availability increases cost. We need to clarify what zero data loss means. Latency also depends on network bandwidth, so 100 ms may not always be achievable.

Communication Diagnosis

  • Strengths
    • Recognized trade-offs instead of accepting requirements blindly.
    • Asked for clarification.
  • Improve
    • Mixed backend latency with end-to-end latency.
    • Could start from business objectives instead of technical limitations.

Architect Articulation Upgrade

Before discussing architecture, I'd like to understand the business priorities behind these requirements. Some quality attributes naturally compete with one another—for example, achieving extremely high availability generally increases infrastructure cost, while guaranteeing zero data loss may introduce additional latency. Once the business priorities are clear, we can evaluate architectural options that provide the right balance.

Why This Sounds Better

  • Begins with business priorities.
  • Explains trade-offs instead of listing them.
  • Uses architect vocabulary.

Interview Takeaway

Never accept or reject NFRs immediately. Validate business priorities first.

Vocabulary Added

  • Business priorities
  • Quality attributes
  • Architectural options
  • Trade-offs

Problem 2 – Slow Food Delivery API

Interview Problem

The ordering API takes three seconds. Your Engineering Manager immediately proposes adding Redis.

Your Original Answer (Mic)

I'd first ask why 500 ms is the target, whether it's benchmarked, and whether the issue occurs in all regions.

Communication Diagnosis

  • Strengths
    • Excellent assumption validation.
    • Did not jump directly to technology.
  • Improve
    • One key question was missing: Where is the latency actually measured?

Architect Articulation Upgrade

Before deciding whether Redis is appropriate, I'd like to understand where the latency is occurring. Is the delay in the client, network, backend, database, or an external dependency? Once we've localized the bottleneck, we can determine whether caching is the correct optimization strategy.

Why This Sounds Better

  • Shows structured reasoning.
  • Demonstrates evidence-based decision making.
  • Avoids assuming the solution.

Interview Takeaway

Localize the bottleneck before proposing an optimization.

Vocabulary Added

  • Localize the bottleneck
  • External dependency
  • Optimization strategy
  • Evidence-based decision

Problem 3 – Photo Upload vs Money Transfer

Interview Problem

Why shouldn't these two APIs use the same architecture?

Your Original Answer (Mic)

Payments are business critical and affect customer trust. Photo uploads can be retried and temporary failures are acceptable. Different Functional Requirements have different Non-Functional Requirements.

Communication Diagnosis

  • Strengths
    • Excellent business-first reasoning.
    • Connected business impact with architecture.
  • Improve
    • State that each Functional Requirement has its own set of NFRs.

Architect Articulation Upgrade

Although both are APIs, their business expectations are fundamentally different. A failed profile-picture upload is usually recoverable with minimal business impact. A failed payment directly affects customer trust, financial correctness, and compliance. Because each Functional Requirement has its own set of Non-Functional Requirements, the resulting architectures are also different.

Why This Sounds Better

  • Connects business impact to technical decisions.
  • Explains the reasoning instead of giving a conclusion.

Interview Takeaway

Architecture follows business expectations—not functionality alone.

Vocabulary Added

  • Business impact
  • Financial correctness
  • Compliance
  • Business expectations
